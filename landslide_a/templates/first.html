Building landslide-resilient communities

The scope of the project comprises of an assessment of landslide hazards based on the monitoring and modelling of these processes in the Kyiv region using integrated techniques. In this project two approaches are used for the assessment and predictions of landslide hazards.

The first one will be based on GIS and statistical analysis of geo-environmental factors related to the occurrence of landslides in the study area.

Landslide hazards in Ukraine

Landslides are the major natural hazard in the Ukraine. Over 23100 landslides were recorded by 2018 in Ukraine with the number of landslides has increased by 1.3 times in the last 15 years, and by 3 times over the last 30 years. It is important for the community to be informed of potential geohazards that may affect their wellbeing. A better understanding of the spatial distribution of landslide hazards will help guide environmental protection policies in the Kyiv region.
